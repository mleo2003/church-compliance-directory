Not legal or tax advice. These guides are operational checklists for planning. Always verify requirements with official agencies and your CPA/attorney.

What every state guide covers

Each guide follows the same structure: - Quick snapshot table — what to track, how often, who owns it - Verified filing points — sourced from official state agencies - Monthly compliance checklist — practical operational rhythm - Common failure patterns — what churches actually miss - Sources — direct links to official agency pages

The problem these guides solve

Most church admins track compliance in one of three ways: 1. A spreadsheet that someone made years ago and nobody maintains 2. A CPA who reminds them once a year (if they're lucky) 3. Memory and good intentions

None of those scale when you have multiple staff, a board that turns over, or volunteers cycling through ministry areas with different requirements.

The purpose of this directory is to make the what and when visible — so church admins can build repeatable workflows instead of scrambling.
