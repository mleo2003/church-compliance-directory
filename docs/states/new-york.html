Church Compliance Deadlines in New York (2026)

This is a directory of publicly available official resources — not legal or tax advice. We link to official state and federal agency pages so church admins can find the right starting point. For advice specific to your situation, consult a licensed attorney or CPA.

New York has one of the more layered compliance environments for nonprofits — the AG Charities Bureau, the Department of State, and the DOL each have separate obligations that can catch church admins off guard.

Who this applies to

  • New York churches operating as nonprofit or religious corporations
  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, board secretaries
  • Churches with employees and recurring payroll obligations

Verified New York filing points

1) CHAR500 annual filing with NY AG Charities Bureau

Organizations registered to solicit charitable contributions in New York must file an annual CHAR500. Deadline: 15th day of the 5th month after fiscal year end for Article 7-A registrants (e.g., May 15 for December 31 fiscal year); last day of the 6th month for EPTL registrants. Extensions of up to 3 months available on written request. Filing is online only at charitiesnys.com (required since September 2022).

Operational takeaway: track your fiscal year end and set reminders 60 days out. Don't assume church exemption without confirming with your CPA/attorney — solicitation activity and asset holdings trigger the requirement.

2) Financial disclosure with NY Department of State

Organizations required to file CHAR500 with the AG and that received more than $250k in gross revenue must also file a financial disclosure report with the NY DOS Division of Corporations by the same deadline. Requires IRS Form 990/990-EZ/990-PF, audited financial statements if revenue exceeds $1M, and Schedule B if applicable.

Operational takeaway: if you're filing CHAR500, confirm whether the DOS parallel filing applies. Same deadline — track together.

3) Biennial statement — nonprofits are EXEMPT

Note: NY DOS biennial statements are required for business corporations and LLCs, not nonprofit or religious corporations. Churches do not file biennial statements with DOS.

4) Unemployment insurance via NY DOL

New York requires employers with 4+ employees for 20+ weeks per year to register for unemployment insurance. Nonprofits can elect the contributory method (standard SUTA tax) or the reimbursable method (self-insure; reimburse state for actual benefits paid). Register via NY Business Express (NYBE) with a Federal EIN.

Operational takeaway: confirm your employee threshold and method election with finance/CPA. Quarterly wage reporting required once registered.

5) Federal church filing nuance still applies

IRS guidance: exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

6) Volunteer screening and mandatory reporting

No statewide mandate for church volunteer background checks (outside licensed childcare). However, all church staff and volunteers are mandatory reporters of suspected child abuse under New York law — reports go to County Child Protective Services. Note: NY law restricts fingerprinting; identification methods must comply with Clean Slate Act.

Monthly church compliance checklist (New York ops)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadline window (CHAR500, DOS financial disclosure, governance)
  • Confirm CHAR500 filing status and deadline based on fiscal year end
  • Verify DOS financial disclosure requirement if revenue >$250k
  • Confirm DOL quarterly wage reporting is on track
  • Capture board minutes and key policy decisions
  • Review volunteer screening and mandatory reporter training status

Common failure patterns

  1. CHAR500 deadline missed — fiscal-year-end timing varies and no automatic reminder
  2. DOS financial disclosure overlooked as a separate requirement from CHAR500
  3. UI contributory vs. reimbursable method election not formally documented
  4. Mandatory reporter obligation not communicated to all staff/volunteers
