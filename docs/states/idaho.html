Verified Idaho filing points

1) Idaho nonprofit annual report cycle

Idaho nonprofit corporations are registered and maintained through the Idaho Secretary of State. Idaho requires nonprofits to file an Annual Report to maintain good standing. The report is due by the last day of the anniversary month of the entity's incorporation — similar to Arizona and Colorado, the due date is entity-specific, not a single statewide deadline.

The annual report must include current officer/director information and registered agent details. Idaho offers online filing through the Secretary of State's online business filing system (SOSbiz). Failure to file can result in administrative dissolution.

Idaho also requires entities to maintain a registered agent with a physical Idaho address at all times.

Note: Idaho does not have a statewide charitable solicitation registration requirement, which simplifies compliance somewhat compared to states like Connecticut or Hawaii.

Operational takeaway: Look up your entity's next annual report due date in the SOSbiz system and set a rolling annual reminder 60 days before your anniversary month.

2) Idaho payroll tax obligations

Idaho employers are required to withhold state income tax and remit it to the Idaho State Tax Commission (STC). Key forms and filings:

  • Form 910: Idaho Withholding Payment Voucher — used for monthly or quarterly withholding deposits
  • Deposit frequency is based on the total withholding liability: annual, quarterly, or monthly
  • Annual W-2 reconciliation (Form 967) is due by January 31, with W-2s submitted to the STC

Unemployment Insurance (UI) is administered by the Idaho Department of Labor (IDOL). Employers file quarterly wage reports and pay UI contributions using Form TAX020. Idaho nonprofit employers may qualify to elect reimbursable employer status for UI rather than paying quarterly contributions — confirm with IDOL.

Operational takeaway: Confirm your withholding deposit schedule with the Idaho STC. Track Form 967 due January 31 as a hard annual deadline. Verify your UI status with the Idaho Department of Labor.

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Operational takeaway: Don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (state + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify any org info changes that require updated filings with Secretary of State
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. Anniversary-month annual report missed because there is no fixed statewide deadline
  3. Form 967 W-2 reconciliation missed or filed late in the January end-of-year crunch
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
