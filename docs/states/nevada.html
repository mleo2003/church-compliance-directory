Verified Nevada filing points

1) Nevada nonprofit registration/reporting cycle

Nevada nonprofits — including churches incorporated as nonprofit corporations — are registered with the Nevada Secretary of State and must file an Annual List of Officers, Directors, and Registered Agent. This filing is due on the last day of the month in which the entity was originally incorporated (the anniversary month). The fee structure varies by entity type.

Nevada requires all corporations to maintain a registered agent with a Nevada street address or a commercial registered agent on file. Failure to maintain a current registered agent or to file the annual list can result in revocation of the entity's charter.

Nevada's Secretary of State uses the SilverFlume online portal for business filings, and nonprofits can manage their annual list and amendments through that system.

Operational takeaway: Nevada's annual list due date is tied to the entity's incorporation month — not a universal calendar date — so each church needs to know its specific anniversary month.

2) Nevada payroll tax obligations

Nevada has no state personal income tax, which means there is no state income tax withholding obligation for employers. However, Nevada does have other payroll-related obligations:

  • Modified Business Tax (MBT): Nevada employers — including churches with employees — are subject to the Modified Business Tax on total gross wages above a quarterly threshold. Churches with payroll should confirm whether they meet the threshold and file quarterly returns with the Nevada Department of Taxation.
  • Unemployment insurance: The Nevada Department of Employment, Training and Rehabilitation (DETR) administers unemployment insurance. Nonprofit employers including churches may be subject to UI contributions or may qualify for reimbursing employer status. Quarterly wage reports and contributions are filed through DETR's employer portal.

Operational takeaway: the absence of personal income tax withholding is not the same as having no payroll compliance obligations — the Modified Business Tax and UI filings still apply.

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Nevada churches holding 501(c)(3) status should confirm whether their specific structure requires a Form 990, 990-EZ, or 990-N (e-Postcard), or whether their entity qualifies for the church exception. Nevada's lack of a state income tax does not affect federal filing obligations.

Operational takeaway: don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (annual list anniversary + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify registered agent and officer information is current in SilverFlume
  • Confirm Modified Business Tax and UI quarterly filing workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. Annual list due dates are missed because the month varies by entity
  3. Churches assume "no income tax = no payroll filings" and miss the Modified Business Tax
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
