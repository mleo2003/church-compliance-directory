Verified Texas points

1) Texas SOS provides nonprofit/business forms and change filings

Texas Secretary of State resources include nonprofit formation and amendment/change forms under the Texas Business Organizations Code.

Operational takeaway: maintain an entity-change checklist (registered agent/office changes, amendments, corrections) and route ownership clearly.

2) Texas Comptroller provides annual franchise tax report-year forms

Texas Comptroller publishes report-year franchise-tax forms/instructions (including current-year report sets).

Operational takeaway: put annual franchise-tax prep/review into your recurring calendar with internal due-before-public-date milestones.

3) Federal church filing nuance still applies

IRS guidance: exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches/some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Operational takeaway: validate your church’s exact federal filing posture yearly; don’t assume one-size-fits-all.

Monthly church compliance checklist (Texas ops)

  • Review next 90-day compliance window
  • Confirm legal entity updates needed (if officers/addresses/registered agent changed)
  • Confirm franchise-tax annual prep status and document ownership
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflows are current
  • Verify governance minutes and key approvals are captured
  • Check volunteer/youth safety screening cadence against policy

Common failure patterns

  1. Entity changes happen but records aren’t updated promptly
  2. Franchise-tax process is left to “once a year panic”
  3. Payroll and governance live in separate systems with no monthly sync
  4. Compliance ownership is implied, not explicit
