Church Compliance Deadlines in Ohio (2026)

This is a directory of publicly available official resources — not legal or tax advice. We link to official state and federal agency pages so church admins can find the right starting point. For advice specific to your situation, consult a licensed attorney or CPA.

Ohio's 5-year entity renewal cycle catches church admins off guard — it's easy to lose track of a deadline that only comes once every five years. This guide surfaces the key recurring obligations.

Who this applies to

  • Ohio churches operating as nonprofit corporations
  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, board secretaries
  • Churches with employees and recurring payroll obligations

Verified Ohio filing points

1) Certificate of Continued Existence — every 5 years

Ohio SOS requires nonprofit corporations to file Form 522 every 5 years within the month of the corporation's anniversary of formation. Fee: $25 standard, $125 expedited (2 business days). Must include corporate name, principal office location, incorporation date, registered agent, and a statement of active engagement. Failure to file results in articles cancellation.

Operational takeaway: track your formation month and set a 6-month advance reminder for the 5-year cycle. This is the most commonly missed Ohio nonprofit obligation.

2) Annual charitable report to Ohio AG (if soliciting)

Ohio AG Charitable Law Section requires registered charities to file an annual report by the 15th day of the 5th month after fiscal year end (e.g., May 15 for December 31 fiscal year). Filing is online only via the charitable registration portal.

Operational takeaway: confirm with your CPA/attorney whether your church's activities require AG registration. If yes, treat this as a hard annual deadline.

3) Unemployment insurance via ODJFS/ERIC

Ohio ODJFS requires nonprofit employers with 5+ employees for 20+ weeks per year to register and file quarterly. Registration and ongoing reporting managed via the ERIC online system. Two methods: contributory (SUTA) or reimbursable (self-insure, may require bond). Quarterly filing mandatory even when no contributions are due.

Operational takeaway: confirm your employee threshold and method election with finance/CPA. Don't skip quarterly filings — required regardless of payment activity.

4) Federal church filing nuance still applies

IRS guidance: exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Operational takeaway: validate your church's exact federal filing posture yearly.

5) Volunteer screening: fingerprint checks provide civil immunity

Ohio law (R.C. § 109.574–.577) provides civil liability immunity to organizations that conduct fingerprint-based background checks for volunteers in unsupervised child-contact positions and a screened volunteer later causes harm. Not a mandate, but strong operational incentive.

Operational takeaway: build fingerprint/background check workflows into your volunteer onboarding for anyone with regular unsupervised child access. Consent required before screening.

Monthly church compliance checklist (Ohio ops)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadline window (SOS 5-year cycle, AG report, governance)
  • Confirm Certificate of Continued Existence status and next due year
  • Verify AG charitable report deadline if applicable
  • Confirm ODJFS quarterly filing is on track
  • Capture board minutes and key decisions
  • Review volunteer screening due dates and consent records

Common failure patterns

  1. 5-year SOS renewal missed — no annual prompt reminds you it's coming
  2. AG annual report deadline missed because fiscal-year-end timing varies
  3. Quarterly ODJFS filing skipped when no payment is due — still required
  4. Volunteer fingerprint program not formalized, losing liability protection
