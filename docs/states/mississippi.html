Verified Mississippi filing points

1) Mississippi nonprofit registration/reporting cycle

Mississippi nonprofit corporations file an Annual Report with the Mississippi Secretary of State. The annual report is due by April 15 of each year.

Key considerations: - Annual reports are filed through the Mississippi Secretary of State's online Business Services portal - The report is required to maintain good standing and must be filed even if no changes have occurred to the organization's information - Failure to file can result in the entity being placed in delinquent status and eventually dissolved - Mississippi has a separate charitable solicitation registration requirement for organizations that solicit contributions from the public — religious organizations are generally exempt under Mississippi law (Miss. Code Ann. § 79-11-503), but verify if your church runs affiliated entities with separate fundraising programs

Operational takeaway: April 15 is Mississippi's fixed annual report deadline for nonprofits. Plan to have the filing completed by early April to allow time for any corrections.

  • Mississippi Secretary of State — Business Services: https://www.sos.ms.gov/Business-Services/Pages/default.aspx
  • Mississippi Secretary of State — Nonprofit Corporations: https://www.sos.ms.gov/Business-Services/Nonprofit-Corporations/Pages/default.aspx

2) Mississippi payroll tax obligations

Mississippi churches with employees must comply with Mississippi Department of Revenue (MDOR) income tax withholding requirements. Mississippi imposes a state income tax on wages, and employers are required to withhold and remit state income tax.

Key agency and forms: - Mississippi Department of Revenue administers state income tax withholding - Employers file Mississippi Employer's Withholding Tax returns — filing frequency (monthly or quarterly) is assigned based on withholding volume - Annual reconciliation and W-2 distribution are required at year end - Mississippi Department of Employment Security (MDES) administers Unemployment Insurance (UI) — religious organizations may have specific exemption or reimbursement election options

Key considerations: - Mississippi religious organization UI exemptions exist but require specific elections — verify your church's status with MDES at setup and whenever organizational structure changes - Mississippi's state income tax rate and withholding tables have changed in recent years as part of ongoing tax reform — confirm current rates with MDOR or your payroll provider

Operational takeaway: Payroll filing should be treated as non-negotiable recurring compliance. Confirm your withholding deposit frequency with MDOR and your UI status with MDES.

  • Mississippi Department of Revenue — Withholding Tax: https://www.dor.ms.gov/Business/Pages/Withholding-Tax.aspx
  • Mississippi Department of Employment Security — Employer UI: https://mdes.ms.gov/employers/

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Specifically: - Churches (as defined by the IRS) are generally not required to file Form 990, 990-EZ, or 990-N - However, church-affiliated organizations (separate nonprofits, foundations, school ministries) may be required to file depending on their structure and gross receipts - Filing a 990-N (e-Postcard) voluntarily is an option some churches use to maintain a public record of activity

Operational takeaway: Don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

  • IRS — Filing Requirements for Churches and Religious Organizations: https://www.irs.gov/charities-non-profits/churches-religious-organizations/filing-requirements-for-churches-and-religious-organizations

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (state + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify any org info changes that require updated filings
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. Missing the April 15 annual report deadline — it aligns with tax season, and church admin can get deprioritized
  3. Payroll and governance tasks treated as separate systems
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
