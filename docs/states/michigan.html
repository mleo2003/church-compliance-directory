Church Compliance Deadlines in Michigan (2026)

Michigan's October 1 annual report deadline and the UI reimbursement election are the two items Michigan church admins most commonly miss. This guide puts both in one place.

Who this applies to

  • Michigan churches operating as nonprofit corporations
  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, board secretaries
  • Churches with employees and recurring payroll obligations

Verified Michigan filing points

1) Annual report — due October 1 each year

Michigan LARA requires nonprofit corporations to file an annual report via the MiBusiness Registry Portal (MiBRP) by October 1 of each year, beginning the year after incorporation. Fee: $20. Form CSCL/CD-2000 is mailed to the registered agent ~3 months before the due date (or email notice if enrolled). Online filing only — paper filings no longer accepted. Non-compliance can result in automatic dissolution if not filed within 2 years of due date.

Operational takeaway: set a July reminder (3 months before Oct 1) to confirm the form arrived and is on track. Don't wait for the preprinted form to start the process.

2) Unemployment insurance — church employees typically exempt

Michigan statute excludes employees of churches and religious organizations from UI coverage. If your church qualifies for the exemption, no registration or quarterly reporting is required. If your church operates a non-exempt subsidiary or separate entity, the standard UIA threshold applies. 501(c)(3) nonprofits that are not exempt can elect the reimbursable method (dollar-for-dollar reimbursement of benefits paid; requires security deposit of 4% of annual taxable payroll if payroll ≥ $100,000) instead of the contributory (SUTA tax) method. Account management via MiWAM portal.

Operational takeaway: confirm exemption status with your CPA/attorney. If reimbursable method is elected, ensure the security deposit requirement is tracked and funded.

3) Federal church filing nuance still applies

IRS guidance: exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted. Validate annually.

4) Volunteer screening — no state mandate, best practice available

Michigan does not mandate background checks for church volunteers. Free ICHAT checks (Michigan criminal convictions only) are available via Michigan State Police. Limitations: does not include federal records, out-of-state history, warrants, or suppressed records. For volunteers without 10+ continuous years in Michigan, a national search is recommended. Safe Gatherings (optional third-party program) provides background checks, references, and abuse prevention training with a 3-year certification.

Operational takeaway: build a written screening policy. Document what check is required for which role, and track renewal dates even without a state mandate.

Monthly church compliance checklist (MI ops)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadline window (annual report, governance)
  • Set July reminder for Oct 1 annual report (confirm form receipt and file early)
  • Confirm UI exemption status or reimbursable method election is on file
  • Verify security deposit adequacy if reimbursable method elected
  • Capture board minutes and key decisions
  • Review volunteer screening policy and any pending renewals

Common failure patterns

  1. Annual report preprinted form arrives and sits unprocessed — Oct 1 deadline missed
  2. UI exemption assumed without CPA confirmation on file
  3. Security deposit requirement for reimbursable method underfunded
  4. Volunteer screening policy not written down — no consistency or audit trail
