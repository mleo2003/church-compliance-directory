Church Compliance Deadlines in Kansas (2026)

This is a directory of publicly available official resources — not legal or tax advice. We link to official state and federal agency pages so church admins can find the right starting point. For advice specific to your situation, consult a licensed attorney or CPA.

If you run admin for a Kansas church, the pain is usually not "what to do" — it's remembering when to do it and who owns it. This draft guide is designed to make that easier.

  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, and board secretaries

Verified Kansas filing points

1) Kansas nonprofit registration/reporting cycle

Kansas nonprofit corporations file an Annual Report with the Kansas Secretary of State. The annual report is due by the 15th day of the sixth month following the close of the organization's fiscal year. For most organizations on a calendar fiscal year, this means the report is due in mid-June.

Key considerations: - Reports can be filed online through the Kansas Business Center - The annual report updates officer and registered agent information on record with the state - Failure to file can result in the nonprofit being placed in delinquent status and eventually administratively dissolved - Kansas also requires nonprofits soliciting charitable contributions to register with the Secretary of State's Charitable Organizations section — churches are generally exempt from charitable solicitation registration under Kansas law, but affiliated foundations or schools may not be

Operational takeaway: Set a recurring calendar reminder well ahead of the annual report due date. If your church has affiliated entities (a school, foundation, or separate ministry), verify their registration status separately.

  • Kansas Secretary of State — Business Entity Annual Reports: https://www.sos.ks.gov/businesses/annual_reports.html
  • Kansas Secretary of State — Nonprofit Corporations: https://www.sos.ks.gov/businesses/nonprofit.html

2) Kansas payroll tax obligations

Kansas churches with employees must comply with Kansas Department of Revenue withholding requirements. Kansas uses a withholding account system and assigns filing frequency based on withholding volume.

Key agency and forms: - Kansas Department of Revenue administers state withholding - Employers file Kansas Withholding Tax returns using KW-3 (annual reconciliation) and periodic deposit returns depending on assigned frequency - Kansas Department of Labor administers Unemployment Insurance (UI) — churches may elect to participate or may be exempt depending on their structure and number of employees

Key considerations: - Kansas churches with fewer than a specified number of employees may be exempt from UI contributions, but this threshold and related elections require verification with Kansas Department of Labor - Withholding deposit frequency is assigned based on your annual withholding liability — confirm your current schedule with KDOR

Operational takeaway: Payroll filing should be treated as non-negotiable recurring compliance. Verify your deposit frequency with KDOR and your UI status with Kansas Department of Labor.

  • Kansas Department of Revenue — Withholding Tax: https://www.ksrevenue.gov/withholdinginfo.html
  • Kansas Department of Labor — Unemployment Insurance: https://www.dol.ks.gov/employers

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Specifically: - Churches (as defined by the IRS) are generally not required to file Form 990, 990-EZ, or 990-N - However, church-affiliated organizations (separate nonprofits, foundations, school ministries) may be required to file depending on their structure and gross receipts - Filing a 990-N (e-Postcard) voluntarily is an option some churches use to maintain a public record of activity

Operational takeaway: Don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

  • IRS — Filing Requirements for Churches and Religious Organizations: https://www.irs.gov/charities-non-profits/churches-religious-organizations/filing-requirements-for-churches-and-religious-organizations

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (state + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify any org info changes that require updated filings
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. "We'll remember later" for state filing windows
  3. Payroll and governance tasks treated as separate systems
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
