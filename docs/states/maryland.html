Verified Maryland filing points

1) Maryland nonprofit registration/reporting cycle

Maryland nonprofit corporations must file an Annual Report and Personal Property Return with the Maryland State Department of Assessments and Taxation (SDAT). This is due by April 15 each year.

Key considerations: - Unlike many states, Maryland's primary recurring filing for nonprofits is the Personal Property Return combined with the Annual Report — this is a more detailed filing than a simple annual report in other states - The return is filed through Maryland Business Express (SDAT's online portal) - Religious organizations may be eligible for a personal property exemption, but the exemption must be affirmatively claimed and documented — it is not automatic - Maryland has a charitable solicitation registration requirement administered by the Office of the Secretary of State — religious organizations are generally exempt under Maryland law (Business Regulation Article § 6-102), but verify your situation, especially if your church has a separately incorporated school or foundation

Operational takeaway: April 15 is the fixed annual deadline for Maryland nonprofits. The combination of annual report and personal property return in a single filing makes this more involved than a simple check-in — allow time for proper preparation, especially to confirm any exemption claims.

  • Maryland SDAT — Nonprofit Corporations: https://dat.maryland.gov/businesses/Pages/Nonprofit-Corporations.aspx
  • Maryland Business Express: https://businessexpress.maryland.gov/

2) Maryland payroll tax obligations

Maryland churches with employees must comply with the Comptroller of Maryland's withholding requirements. Maryland has both state and local (county) income tax withholding, making it one of the more complex payroll states for employers.

Key agency and forms: - Comptroller of Maryland administers state and local income tax withholding - Employers file Maryland Employer Withholding returns — filing frequency (monthly, quarterly, accelerated) is assigned based on withholding volume - An annual withholding reconciliation (MW508) is required, along with W-2 distribution - Maryland Department of Labor, Division of Unemployment Insurance administers UI contributions — religious organizations may have reimbursement election options

Key considerations: - Maryland's local income tax (county tax) is withheld by employers along with state income tax — this requires knowing the employee's county of residence - The combined state + county withholding requirement makes Maryland payroll more complex than average; a payroll system or CPA familiar with Maryland is strongly recommended - Maryland UI: religious organizations may elect to reimburse the state for UI benefits paid rather than paying quarterly contributions — verify election status and renewal requirements

Operational takeaway: Maryland's local income tax layer makes payroll particularly important to get right. Work with a payroll provider experienced in Maryland compliance.

  • Comptroller of Maryland — Employer Withholding: https://www.marylandtaxes.gov/business/income/withholding/index.php
  • Maryland Department of Labor — Unemployment Insurance: https://www.labor.maryland.gov/employment/unemployment.shtml

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Specifically: - Churches (as defined by the IRS) are generally not required to file Form 990, 990-EZ, or 990-N - However, church-affiliated organizations (separate nonprofits, foundations, school ministries) may be required to file depending on their structure and gross receipts - Filing a 990-N (e-Postcard) voluntarily is an option some churches use to maintain a public record of activity

Operational takeaway: Don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

  • IRS — Filing Requirements for Churches and Religious Organizations: https://www.irs.gov/charities-non-profits/churches-religious-organizations/filing-requirements-for-churches-and-religious-organizations

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (state + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify any org info changes that require updated filings
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. Missing the April 15 personal property return / annual report — or filing without claiming an eligible exemption
  3. Payroll local tax layer overlooked entirely
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
