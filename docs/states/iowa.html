Verified Iowa filing points

1) Iowa nonprofit registration/reporting cycle

Iowa nonprofit corporations file a Biennial Report with the Iowa Secretary of State. The report is due every two years, with the filing window and due date tied to the organization's registration anniversary. Iowa's biennial report system means the cycle is two years rather than annual — but that also means it's easy to lose track of.

Key considerations: - Reports are filed through the Iowa Secretary of State's online portal (Fast Track Filing) - Failure to file can result in administrative dissolution - If organizational information changes (registered agent, principal office, officers), an amended filing may be required outside the biennial cycle

Operational takeaway: Set a two-year recurring reminder at least 60 days before the biennial due date. Don't let the longer cycle create a false sense of security — dissolution for non-filing still happens.

  • Iowa Secretary of State — Nonprofit Corporations: https://sos.iowa.gov/business/nonprofitcorporations.html
  • Iowa Fast Track Filing portal: https://sos.iowa.gov/business/FastTrackFiling.html

2) Iowa payroll tax obligations

Iowa churches with employees must comply with Iowa Department of Revenue withholding requirements. Iowa uses a withholding permit system, and the deposit and filing frequency is determined by the amount withheld.

Key agency and forms: - Iowa Department of Revenue administers state withholding (Iowa Income Tax Withholding) - Employers file Iowa Withholding Tax Quarterly Return (Form 44-095) or may have a different schedule depending on withholding amounts - Iowa also has a Unemployment Insurance program administered by Iowa Workforce Development — churches that have elected coverage or have qualifying employees must comply

Key considerations: - Iowa exempts certain religious organizations from UI contributions in some circumstances, but this requires a formal election and ongoing compliance; verify your exact status with Iowa Workforce Development - Payroll deposits must be made on the schedule determined by your withholding volume — do not assume quarterly is always correct

Operational takeaway: Payroll filing should be treated as non-negotiable recurring compliance. Verify your deposit frequency with Iowa DOR and your UI status with Iowa Workforce Development.

  • Iowa Department of Revenue — Withholding Tax: https://tax.iowa.gov/businesses/withholding
  • Iowa Workforce Development — Unemployment Insurance: https://www.iowaworkforcedevelopment.gov/unemployment-insurance-tax

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Specifically: - Churches (as defined by the IRS) are generally not required to file Form 990, 990-EZ, or 990-N - However, church-affiliated organizations (separate nonprofits, foundations, school ministries) may be required to file depending on their structure and gross receipts - Filing a 990-N (e-Postcard) voluntarily is an option some churches use to maintain a public record of activity

Operational takeaway: Don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

  • IRS — Filing Requirements for Churches and Religious Organizations: https://www.irs.gov/charities-non-profits/churches-religious-organizations/filing-requirements-for-churches-and-religious-organizations

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (state + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify any org info changes that require updated filings
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. "We'll remember later" for state filing windows — especially dangerous with Iowa's biennial cycle
  3. Payroll and governance tasks treated as separate systems
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
