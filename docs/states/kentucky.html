Church Compliance Deadlines in Kentucky (2026)

This is a directory of publicly available official resources — not legal or tax advice. We link to official state and federal agency pages so church admins can find the right starting point. For advice specific to your situation, consult a licensed attorney or CPA.

If you run admin for a Kentucky church, the pain is usually not "what to do" — it's remembering when to do it and who owns it. This draft guide is designed to make that easier.

  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, and board secretaries
  • Churches with employees and recurring payroll obligations

Verified Kentucky filing points

1) Kentucky nonprofit registration/reporting cycle

Kentucky nonprofit corporations file an Annual Report with the Kentucky Secretary of State. The annual report is due by June 30 each year, regardless of the organization's fiscal year end.

Key considerations: - Annual reports are filed online through the Kentucky Secretary of State's business portal - The report updates officer and registered agent information on the state's records - Failure to file the annual report will result in the entity being placed in bad standing, which can lead to administrative dissolution - Kentucky requires charitable organizations soliciting donations to register with the Attorney General's Office of Consumer Protection — churches are generally exempt from this charitable solicitation registration under Kentucky law (KRS Chapter 367), but verify if your church conducts broad public solicitation or has affiliated entities

Operational takeaway: June 30 is a fixed annual deadline for all Kentucky nonprofits. Set a recurring reminder in March or April to allow adequate prep time.

  • Kentucky Secretary of State — Business Filings: https://www.sos.ky.gov/bus/business/Pages/default.aspx
  • Kentucky One Stop Business Portal: https://onestop.ky.gov/

2) Kentucky payroll tax obligations

Kentucky churches with employees must comply with Kentucky Department of Revenue withholding requirements. Kentucky has a flat individual income tax rate, and employer withholding is required for resident and nonresident employee wages.

Key agency and forms: - Kentucky Department of Revenue administers state income tax withholding - Employers file Kentucky Withholding Tax returns — filing frequency (monthly, quarterly, or annually) is assigned based on withholding volume - Kentucky Career Center / Office of Unemployment Insurance administers UI contributions — religious organizations may qualify for a reimbursement option rather than the standard contribution method

Key considerations: - Kentucky's local occupational license taxes (city and county level) may apply to church employees depending on the jurisdiction — this is often overlooked; confirm with your local government - Some Kentucky cities (including Louisville and Lexington) have local wage taxes that require separate employer registration and filing

Operational takeaway: Kentucky payroll has both state and potentially local layers. Confirm both levels of compliance with your payroll provider or CPA.

  • Kentucky Department of Revenue — Withholding Tax: https://revenue.ky.gov/Business/Withholding-Tax/Pages/default.aspx
  • Kentucky Office of Unemployment Insurance: https://kcc.ky.gov/employer/Pages/default.aspx

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Specifically: - Churches (as defined by the IRS) are generally not required to file Form 990, 990-EZ, or 990-N - However, church-affiliated organizations (separate nonprofits, foundations, school ministries) may be required to file depending on their structure and gross receipts - Filing a 990-N (e-Postcard) voluntarily is an option some churches use to maintain a public record of activity

Operational takeaway: Don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

  • IRS — Filing Requirements for Churches and Religious Organizations: https://www.irs.gov/charities-non-profits/churches-religious-organizations/filing-requirements-for-churches-and-religious-organizations

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (state + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify any org info changes that require updated filings
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. Missing the June 30 Kentucky annual report deadline — it catches churches off guard mid-summer
  3. Payroll and governance tasks treated as separate systems
  4. Local occupational tax obligations overlooked entirely
