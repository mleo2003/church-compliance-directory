Illinois has a mandatory DCFS Mandated Reporter training requirement for all church staff and volunteers working with children — one of the more actionable compliance items a church admin can own directly without an attorney.

Who this applies to

  • Illinois churches operating as nonprofit corporations
  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, board secretaries
  • Churches with employees, payroll, and volunteers working with children

Verified Illinois filing points

1) NFP annual report — before incorporation anniversary month

Illinois Secretary of State requires nonprofit corporations (NFP) to file an annual report before the first day of the month in which the corporation was incorporated. Form: NFP 114.05. Filed online at apps.ilsos.gov/nfpannualreports. No fee. Requires current officers and directors list. Non-compliance can result in involuntary dissolution.

Operational takeaway: track your incorporation month, not just December 31. A church incorporated in October files before October 1 each year.

2) DCFS Mandated Reporter training — required for all child-facing staff/volunteers

Effective January 1, 2020, Illinois law requires all staff and volunteers who work with children in churches to complete DCFS free online Mandated Reporter Training. Duration: 60–90 minutes, self-paced. New staff/volunteers must complete within 3 months of starting. Renewal: every 3 years. Free at mr.dcfstraining.org. Completion certificate must be printed/saved.

Operational takeaway: this is the most directly actionable compliance requirement for most Illinois churches. Requires a tracking system with start dates and renewal dates per person. This is exactly the kind of task Compliance Calendar is designed to operationalize.

3) Unemployment insurance — churches typically exempt

Illinois UI law excludes churches operated primarily for religious purposes (under 4 employees). If your church has 4+ employees for 20+ weeks per year and does not qualify for the religious exemption, quarterly reporting via IDES (Form UI-3/40) is required. Verify exemption status with your CPA/attorney.

4) Federal church filing nuance still applies

IRS guidance: exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted. Validate annually.

Monthly church compliance checklist (IL ops)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadline window (NFP annual report, DCFS training renewals, governance)
  • Confirm NFP annual report is on track for pre-anniversary-month deadline
  • Run DCFS training renewal report: identify anyone due for renewal within 90 days
  • Verify new staff/volunteers are scheduled for training within 3 months of start
  • Confirm UI exemption status on file
  • Capture board minutes and key decisions

Common failure patterns

  1. NFP annual report due date confused with December 31 — each church has a different anniversary month
  2. DCFS training treated as one-time onboarding rather than renewable obligation
  3. New volunteers begin child contact before completing DCFS training
  4. UI exemption assumed without documentation on file
