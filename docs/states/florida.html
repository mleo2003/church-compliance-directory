Verified Florida points

1) Annual report is required to maintain active status

Florida Division of Corporations states annual report filing is required each year to maintain active status, whether changes are needed or not.

2) Late/administrative consequences are explicit

The same page states: - For-profit entities/LLCs etc. face late-fee consequences after May 1 - Nonprofit corporations are not subject to the $400 late fee - Failure to file by statutory September deadlines can result in administrative dissolution/revocation

Operational takeaway: set reminders well before May 1 and maintain a hard fail-safe reminder before September statutory cutoffs.

3) Florida reemployment tax obligations are clear

Florida DOR states employers pay reemployment tax and file related wage/tax reporting; core rules and workflows are maintained on DOR pages.

Operational takeaway: payroll/reemployment compliance should be part of the same recurring checklist, not a separate silo.

4) Federal church filing nuance still applies

IRS notes exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches/some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Monthly church compliance checklist (Florida ops)

  • Review next 90-day deadline set
  • Confirm annual report status and owner accountability
  • Verify payroll + reemployment reporting flow
  • Confirm board records and policy decisions are documented
  • Review youth safety screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns

  1. Annual report postponed because “no changes this year”
  2. Payroll and church-admin compliance treated as separate systems
  3. No escalation path before statutory September risk window
  4. Owners unclear, so tasks drift until urgent
