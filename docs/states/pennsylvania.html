Church Compliance Deadlines in Pennsylvania (2026)

This is a directory of publicly available official resources — not legal or tax advice. We link to official state and federal agency pages so church admins can find the right starting point. For advice specific to your situation, consult a licensed attorney or CPA.

Pennsylvania has one of the most operationally demanding volunteer screening requirements in the country — Act 153 mandates three-clearance packages for anyone with child contact. Church admins who don't have a system for this face real liability exposure.

Who this applies to

  • Pennsylvania churches operating as nonprofit corporations
  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, board secretaries
  • Churches with employees, payroll, and volunteers working with children

Verified Pennsylvania filing points

1) Annual report — new requirement effective 2025

As of 2025, Pennsylvania nonprofit corporations must file an annual report with the PA Department of State Bureau of Corporations & Charitable Organizations. Due by June 30 each year. Churches formed before 2025: first filing due June 30, 2025. Churches formed in 2025: first filing due June 30, 2026. Filing is online at file.dos.pa.gov. No fee for nonprofits. Non-compliance can result in administrative dissolution (dissolution penalties phased in starting 2027).

Operational takeaway: this replaces the old decennial filing. Set an annual June 30 reminder — don't treat this as a one-time task.

2) Act 153 — three-clearance requirement for child-contact volunteers

Pennsylvania Act 153 (2014) requires ALL volunteers (paid and unpaid) with child contact to obtain three clearances, renewed every 60 months (5 years): - PA Child Abuse History Clearance (DHS) — via CWIS portal; fee waived once per 57-month cycle for volunteers - PA Criminal History Clearance (PATCH) — PA State Police - FBI Identity History Summary (fingerprint) — required for new volunteers and those who haven't lived continuously in PA for 10 years

Operational takeaway: this is the highest-stakes compliance item for most churches. Requires a tracking system with renewal dates per volunteer. A Sunday school teacher who started 5 years ago is due for renewal now. No system = no visibility = real liability.

3) Unemployment compensation — religious organizations typically exempt

PA UC Law excludes 501(c)(3) churches and nonprofits operated exclusively or primarily for religious purposes. If your church does not meet the religious exemption, the reimbursable method (dollar-for-dollar reimbursement of benefits paid) is available as an alternative to SUTA tax. Verify your exemption status with your CPA/attorney.

4) Federal church filing nuance still applies

IRS guidance: exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted. Validate annually.

Monthly church compliance checklist (PA ops)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadline window (annual report, Act 153 renewals, governance)
  • Confirm annual report filed or on track for June 30
  • Run Act 153 renewal report: identify any volunteer whose clearance expires within 90 days
  • Verify new volunteer onboarding includes all three clearances before first contact with children
  • Confirm payroll/UC exemption status on file
  • Capture board minutes and key decisions

Common failure patterns

  1. Act 153 renewals tracked in spreadsheets with no automated alerts — clearances lapse
  2. New annual report requirement (2025+) not yet in admin calendar
  3. New volunteers allowed child contact before clearances are complete
  4. Religious exemption for UC assumed without formal documentation on file
