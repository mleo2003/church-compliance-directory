Church Compliance Deadlines in Oregon (2026)

This is a directory of publicly available official resources — not legal or tax advice. We link to official state and federal agency pages so church admins can find the right starting point. For advice specific to your situation, consult a licensed attorney or CPA.

If you run admin for an Oregon church, the pain is usually not "what to do" — it's remembering when to do it and who owns it. This draft guide is designed to make that easier.

Who this applies to

  • Oregon churches that operate as nonprofit entities
  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, and board secretaries
  • Churches with employees and recurring payroll obligations

Verified Oregon filing points

1) Oregon nonprofit registration/reporting cycle

Oregon nonprofits incorporated as nonprofit corporations must file an annual report with the Oregon Secretary of State. The annual report is due each year by the anniversary of the registration date. It updates the entity's registered agent, principal office address, and officer/director information. Filings are made through the Oregon Business Registry (OBR) online portal.

Oregon also requires charitable organizations that solicit charitable contributions from the public to register with the Oregon Department of Justice, Charitable Activities Section. Annual financial reports are required for registered charities above certain revenue thresholds. Churches are generally exempt from the registration requirement under ORS 128.620(1), but the exemption has specific criteria based on how contributions are solicited and how the organization is governed. Each Oregon church should confirm whether the exemption applies to their specific activities.

Operational takeaway: Oregon churches face an annual report to the Secretary of State and should separately evaluate whether the DOJ Charitable Activities registration requirement applies or an exemption covers their activities.

2) Oregon payroll tax obligations

Oregon has a comprehensive payroll tax environment. Churches with employees must manage multiple obligations:

  • State income tax withholding: Employers must withhold Oregon income tax and remit it to the Oregon Department of Revenue on a schedule based on withholding volume (monthly or quarterly). Annual reconciliation is required.
  • Unemployment insurance (UI): The Oregon Employment Department administers UI. Nonprofit employers may elect reimbursing employer status as an alternative to the contribution rate system. Quarterly wage reports and contributions are filed through the Frances Online system.
  • Statewide Transit Tax (STT): Oregon imposes a statewide transit tax withheld from employee wages. Employers must withhold and remit the STT to the Department of Revenue quarterly.
  • Paid Family and Medical Leave Insurance (PFMLI): Oregon's Paid Leave Oregon program requires most employers to contribute and withhold from employees. Quarterly contributions are filed through the Frances Online system. Small employers (fewer than 25 employees) may be exempt from the employer contribution portion but still must withhold the employee portion.

Operational takeaway: Oregon has one of the more complex state payroll environments — withholding, UI, Statewide Transit Tax, and Paid Leave Oregon are all separate obligations with different schedules.

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Oregon churches holding 501(c)(3) status should confirm whether their specific structure requires a Form 990, 990-EZ, or 990-N (e-Postcard), or whether their entity qualifies for the church exception. Oregon does not impose a separate state corporate income tax on qualifying nonprofits, but this does not eliminate federal filing obligations.

Operational takeaway: don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (annual report anniversary + DOJ charitable registration + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify registered agent and officer information is current with Secretary of State
  • Confirm withholding, UI, STT, and Paid Leave Oregon quarterly workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. The Statewide Transit Tax and Paid Leave Oregon are missed because they are newer obligations
  3. DOJ Charitable Activities registration status is not confirmed — churches assume blanket exemption
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
