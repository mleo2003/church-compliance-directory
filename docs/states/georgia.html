Church Compliance Deadlines in Georgia (2026)

Georgia church admins face a common pattern: annual entity filings slip because "nothing changed," and payroll obligations get siloed from the rest of compliance. This guide is designed to make the calendar visible.

Who this applies to

  • Georgia churches operating as nonprofit corporations
  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, board secretaries
  • Churches with employees and recurring payroll obligations

Verified Georgia filing points

1) Annual registration is required each year (Jan 1 – Apr 1)

Georgia Secretary of State requires nonprofit corporations to file an annual registration each year during the Jan 1 – Apr 1 window. Filing fee: $30. Must include at least 3 principal officers and a registered agent with a physical Georgia street address.

Operational takeaway: set a January reminder; don't wait for Apr 1.

2) Charitable registration renewal if soliciting donations

If your church publicly solicits donations (beyond normal member giving), Georgia SOS Charities Division requires biennial renewal. Renewal window opens 90 days before expiration. Two years of financial reporting required with renewal. Fee: $20.

Operational takeaway: verify whether your church's activity triggers this requirement with your CPA/attorney. If it does, track the expiration date as a hard deadline.

3) Unemployment insurance (DOL-1N) if 4+ employees

Georgia DOL requires nonprofit employers with 4+ employees for 20+ weeks per year to register using paper Form DOL-1N (online registration not available for nonprofits). Attach IRS 501(c)(3) determination letter. Two methods available: contributory (SUTA tax) or reimbursable (self-insure; may require bond/deposit).

Operational takeaway: confirm your employee threshold and method election with finance/CPA. Quarterly tax/wage reporting required once registered.

4) Federal church filing nuance still applies

IRS guidance: exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Operational takeaway: validate your church's exact federal filing posture yearly — don't assume universal exemption.

5) Volunteer screening: recommended, not mandated

Georgia does not mandate background checks for all church volunteers. However, federal requirements apply if operating licensed childcare. Best practice: require screening (state criminal registry, sex offender registry, child abuse/neglect registry check) for any volunteer with child access.

Monthly church compliance checklist (Georgia ops)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadline window (SOS, DOL, governance)
  • Confirm annual registration is filed before Apr 1
  • Verify charitable registration renewal status if applicable
  • Confirm payroll/unemployment quarterly reporting is on track
  • Capture board minutes and key policy decisions
  • Review volunteer screening due dates against policy

Common failure patterns

  1. Annual registration missed because "nothing changed this year" — still required
  2. Charitable registration renewal overlooked; expiration date not tracked
  3. DOL registration delayed because paper-only process is unfamiliar
  4. Volunteer screening treated as one-time, not recurring
