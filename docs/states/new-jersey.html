Verified New Jersey filing points

1) New Jersey nonprofit registration/reporting cycle

New Jersey nonprofits incorporated under the New Jersey Nonprofit Corporation Act must file an annual report with the New Jersey Department of the Treasury, Division of Revenue and Enterprise Services. The annual report is due on the last day of the month in which the anniversary of the incorporation falls. The filing keeps officer, registered agent, and address information current.

In addition, New Jersey requires charities that solicit contributions in the state to register with the New Jersey Division of Consumer Affairs, Charities Registration Section (under the Office of the Attorney General). Churches are generally exempt from New Jersey charity registration requirements under N.J.S.A. 45:17A-26, but this exemption has specific conditions and some affiliated entities (such as church foundations or separate fundraising arms) may not qualify. Each entity should confirm its status.

New Jersey also requires nonprofits to have a registered agent in New Jersey.

Operational takeaway: New Jersey churches face at least one annual filing with Treasury and should separately confirm their charity registration status with the AG's office.

2) New Jersey payroll tax obligations

New Jersey has one of the more complex state payroll environments. Churches with employees must manage several obligations through the New Jersey Division of Taxation and the New Jersey Department of Labor and Workforce Development:

  • Income tax withholding: Employers must withhold New Jersey gross income tax from employee wages and remit on a schedule based on total withholding liability (monthly, quarterly, or weekly). Annual reconciliation (Form NJ-W-3) is required.
  • Unemployment insurance (UI): The New Jersey Department of Labor and Workforce Development administers UI. Most employers, including nonprofits, are subject to UI contributions; nonprofits may qualify for reimbursing employer status.
  • State Disability Insurance (SDI) and Family Leave Insurance (FLI): New Jersey requires employers to participate in the state SDI and FLI programs. Contributions are withheld from employee wages and the employer also has obligations. These are administered through the Department of Labor and Workforce Development.

Operational takeaway: New Jersey payroll compliance involves withholding, UI, SDI, and FLI — churches should confirm they are registered and current on all four programs.

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

New Jersey churches holding 501(c)(3) status should confirm whether their specific structure requires a Form 990, 990-EZ, or 990-N (e-Postcard), or whether their entity qualifies for the church exception. New Jersey does not impose a separate state corporate income tax on qualifying nonprofits, but this does not eliminate federal filing obligations.

Operational takeaway: don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (annual report anniversary + AG registration + payroll cycle + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify registered agent and officer information is current with NJ Treasury
  • Confirm payroll withholding, UI, SDI, and FLI workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. The AG charity registration issue is not investigated — churches assume blanket exemption without confirming
  3. SDI and FLI obligations are missed because they are less visible than income tax withholding
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
