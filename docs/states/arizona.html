Verified Arizona filing points

1) Arizona nonprofit annual report cycle

Arizona nonprofit corporations are governed by the Arizona Nonprofit Corporation Act and registered through the Arizona Corporation Commission (ACC). Arizona requires nonprofit corporations to file an Annual Report to maintain good standing. The report is due during the anniversary month of the entity's incorporation — not on a fixed calendar date — which means each church has its own annual due date.

The annual report must include current officer and director information and any changes to the registered agent. Arizona has moved much of its corporate filing online through the ACC eCorp portal. Failure to file can result in administrative dissolution.

Churches operating as unincorporated associations or under a denomination's charter structure should verify whether a separate Arizona entity requires an annual report.

Operational takeaway: Look up your entity's anniversary month in the ACC eCorp portal and set a rolling annual reminder 60–90 days before that month each year.

2) Arizona payroll tax obligations

Arizona employers are required to withhold state income tax from employee wages. Key forms include:

  • Form A1-QRT: Arizona Quarterly Withholding Tax Return — filed quarterly
  • Form A1-R: Arizona Annual Payment Withholding Tax Return — annual reconciliation, typically due January 31 for the prior year, with W-2s attached

Withholding deposit frequency (quarterly, monthly, or accelerated) is determined by the total withholding amount. The Arizona Department of Revenue (ADOR) administers withholding compliance.

Unemployment insurance (UI) in Arizona is administered by the Department of Economic Security (DES). Employers file quarterly wage reports and pay UI taxes. Nonprofit employers, including churches, may qualify to elect reimbursable employer status for UI rather than paying quarterly contributions — contact DES for details.

Operational takeaway: Confirm your withholding deposit schedule with ADOR based on your payroll volume. Verify your UI status with DES — the reimbursable election can be financially significant for churches.

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Operational takeaway: Don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (state + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify any org info changes that require updated filings with ACC
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. Anniversary-month annual reports get missed because there's no fixed calendar deadline
  3. Payroll and governance tasks treated as separate systems
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
