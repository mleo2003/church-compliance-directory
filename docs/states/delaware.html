Church Compliance Deadlines in Delaware (2026)

This is a directory of publicly available official resources — not legal or tax advice. We link to official state and federal agency pages so church admins can find the right starting point. For advice specific to your situation, consult a licensed attorney or CPA.

If you run admin for a Delaware church, the pain is usually not "what to do" — it's remembering when to do it and who owns it. This draft guide is designed to make that easier.

Who this applies to

  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, and board secretaries
  • Churches with employees and recurring payroll obligations

Verified Delaware filing points

1) Delaware nonprofit annual report and franchise tax cycle

Delaware is one of the most widely used states for nonprofit incorporation due to its well-developed corporate law framework. Delaware nonprofit corporations are registered through the Delaware Division of Corporations (part of the Department of State). Nonprofits must file an Annual Report due March 1 each year.

Unlike Delaware for-profit corporations, qualifying Delaware nonprofits may not owe a franchise tax — however, the annual report filing itself is still required. The filing can be completed online through the Delaware Division of Corporations' online filing portal. The fee for the annual report varies by entity type.

A critical and ongoing Delaware requirement is maintaining a registered agent with a physical address in Delaware. Delaware requires all entities to have a registered agent at all times. Many churches use a professional registered agent service because Delaware-incorporated churches often have no physical presence in the state.

Operational takeaway: The March 1 annual report deadline is fixed and firm. Set a February reminder every year. Confirm your registered agent information is current and that your agent has not changed contact arrangements.

2) Delaware payroll tax obligations

Delaware employers are required to withhold state income tax from employee wages. Key forms and filings:

  • Form W-1: Delaware Employer's Monthly Withholding Return — for monthly deposit filers
  • Form W-1A: Delaware Employer's Quarterly Withholding Return — for quarterly filers
  • Deposit frequency depends on total withholding liability
  • Annual W-2 reconciliation is required, with W-2s due to the Delaware Division of Revenue

Unemployment Insurance (UI) in Delaware is administered by the Delaware Department of Labor, Division of Unemployment Insurance. Employers file quarterly wage reports (Form UC-1) and pay UI contributions. Delaware nonprofit employers may elect reimbursable employer status for UI — contact the Division of Unemployment Insurance for details.

Note: Churches that are incorporated in Delaware but operate primarily in another state also have payroll obligations in the state where employees actually work. Delaware incorporation does not replace the filing obligations in the state of actual operations.

Operational takeaway: Payroll compliance follows where employees work, not just where the entity is incorporated. Confirm your withholding and UI accounts are established in Delaware if you have Delaware-based employees.

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Operational takeaway: Don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (state + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify registered agent is current and responsive
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. March 1 annual report deadline missed — often because it's early in the year before Q1 rhythm is established
  3. Registered agent relationship goes stale, causing notice failures
  4. Churches incorporated in Delaware assume Delaware filings cover their operational state obligations
