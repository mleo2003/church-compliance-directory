Church Compliance Deadlines in Utah (2026)

This is a directory of publicly available official resources — not legal or tax advice. We link to official state and federal agency pages so church admins can find the right starting point. For advice specific to your situation, consult a licensed attorney or CPA.

Utah has a notably active religious community and a generally business-friendly regulatory environment. For church administrators, the compliance picture involves the Utah Division of Corporations' annual renewal system, Utah State Tax Commission payroll withholding requirements, and Department of Workforce Services unemployment insurance obligations. Utah's streamlined online filing portal makes it easier than many states to stay current — but "easier to file" also means fewer excuses when deadlines slip. Churches that have never missed a filing often get caught when a staff transition leaves no clear owner for the renewal.

Who this applies to

  • Utah churches that operate as nonprofit entities
  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, and board secretaries
  • Churches with employees and recurring payroll obligations

Verified Utah filing points

1) Utah nonprofit registration/reporting cycle

Utah nonprofit corporations must file an annual renewal with the Utah Division of Corporations and Commercial Code (DCCC). Unlike some states that use a fixed annual deadline, Utah ties the renewal due date to the anniversary month of the organization's incorporation. The renewal is filed online through Utah's One Stop Business Registration system and requires payment of a renewal fee. Failure to renew results in administrative dissolution after a defined delinquency period.

Utah has a Charitable Solicitation Act administered through the Division of Consumer Protection. Religious organizations are generally exempt from registration under Utah Code if they solicit funds primarily from their own members and the religious function is not incidental to solicitation. However, organizations that conduct broader public fundraising campaigns, or that are structured so that charitable solicitation is a primary activity, may be required to register. The registration year and renewal timing are set by the Division of Consumer Protection.

Churches should confirm registered agent information is current with the Division of Corporations. Agent changes require a formal amendment filing.

Operational takeaway: Identify your church's incorporation anniversary month and build the annual renewal reminder 60 days in advance. Separately, document your solicitation activities and confirm whether the religious organization exemption from charitable solicitation registration applies.

2) Utah payroll tax obligations

Utah employers, including churches with paid staff, are required to withhold Utah state income tax from employee wages and remit to the Utah State Tax Commission. The primary withholding return is Form TC-941 (Utah Withholding Return). Filing frequency — monthly, quarterly, or annual — depends on the volume of withholding. Churches withholding larger amounts file monthly; smaller withholders file quarterly. Annual reconciliation is submitted with Form TC-941R.

Utah employers must also register with the Utah Department of Workforce Services for state unemployment insurance (SUI). Religious organizations in Utah may be eligible to elect reimbursing employer status, paying actual unemployment claims directly rather than quarterly SUI contributions. Quarterly wage reports are due on the last day of the month following each calendar quarter.

Utah also has a workers' compensation requirement for employers with employees — this is handled through private insurance or the state's workers' compensation fund, not through a state tax filing, but it is a compliance obligation that church admins must not overlook.

Operational takeaway: Confirm your TC-941 filing frequency with the Utah State Tax Commission and build quarterly reminders for DWS unemployment reports. If your church has not evaluated the reimbursing employer election for SUI, review it with your CPA.

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Operational takeaway: Don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (state + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify any org info changes that require updated filings
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. "We'll remember later" for state filing windows
  3. Payroll and governance tasks treated as separate systems
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
