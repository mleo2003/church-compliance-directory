Church Compliance Deadlines in Arkansas (2026)

This is a directory of publicly available official resources — not legal or tax advice. We link to official state and federal agency pages so church admins can find the right starting point. For advice specific to your situation, consult a licensed attorney or CPA.

If you run admin for an Arkansas church, the pain is usually not "what to do" — it's remembering when to do it and who owns it. This draft guide is designed to make that easier.

Who this applies to

  • Church admins, executive pastors, finance teams, and board secretaries

Verified Arkansas filing points

1) Arkansas nonprofit annual franchise tax report and filing cycle

Arkansas nonprofit corporations registered with the Arkansas Secretary of State are required to file an Annual Franchise Tax Report to maintain good standing. This report is due May 1 each year. While many nonprofits (including churches) may owe $0 in franchise tax, the report itself must still be filed to keep the entity active.

The Arkansas Secretary of State's online filing portal (Arkansas Secretary of State Business and Commercial Services) allows entities to file the annual report electronically. Failure to file by May 1 results in a late penalty and can lead to administrative dissolution of the entity.

Churches should also maintain a current registered agent in Arkansas. Any changes to the registered agent or registered office must be reported to the Secretary of State.

Note: Arkansas also has a Charitable Organization Registration requirement through the Arkansas Attorney General's office for organizations soliciting charitable contributions. Certain religious organizations are exempt, but the exemption should be verified.

Operational takeaway: Put May 1 on your compliance calendar every year. Even with a $0 tax balance, the report must be filed. Set a 60-day advance reminder.

2) Arkansas payroll tax obligations

Arkansas employers are required to withhold state income tax and remit it to the Arkansas Department of Finance and Administration (DFA). Key forms and filings include:

  • Form AR941: Arkansas Employer's Quarterly Withholding Tax Return — filed quarterly
  • Monthly deposit requirements apply to employers with larger withholding amounts
  • Annual W-2 reconciliation: Employers must file W-2s and a reconciliation with DFA, typically due by February 28 following the tax year

Unemployment Insurance (UI) is administered by the Arkansas Division of Workforce Services (DWS). Employers with covered employees file quarterly wage reports and pay UI contributions. Nonprofit employers, including churches, may have the option to elect reimbursable employer status for UI — contact DWS for details.

Operational takeaway: Confirm your withholding deposit schedule with DFA. Verify your UI contribution status with the Arkansas Division of Workforce Services. Track the annual W-2/reconciliation deadline separately from quarterly returns.

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Operational takeaway: Don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (state + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify any org info changes that require updated filings
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflow is on track
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. May 1 franchise tax report deadline missed because the entity assumes $0 tax = no filing required
  3. Payroll and governance tasks treated as separate systems
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
