Verified Connecticut filing points

1) Connecticut nonprofit annual report and charitable registration cycle

Connecticut nonprofit corporations are registered through the Connecticut Secretary of the State (SOTS). Nonprofits must file an Annual Report due by the last day of the anniversary month of the entity's incorporation. The report must include current officer/director information and registered agent details. Connecticut offers online filing through the Business Registry portal.

Connecticut also maintains a Charitable Organization Registration requirement administered by the Connecticut Department of Consumer Protection. Organizations soliciting charitable contributions from Connecticut residents must register and renew annually. The registration renewal deadline is tied to the organization's fiscal year end. While religious organizations are often exempt from charitable solicitation registration under Connecticut General Statutes, the exemption has specific parameters — confirm whether your church's fundraising activities qualify for the exemption.

Operational takeaway: Track both the SOTS annual report (anniversary month) and any charitable solicitation registration renewal separately — these are different agencies and different deadlines.

2) Connecticut payroll tax obligations

Connecticut employers have a multi-layer payroll compliance obligation:

State income tax withholding is administered by the Connecticut Department of Revenue Services (DRS): - Form CT-941: Connecticut Quarterly Reconciliation of Withholding — filed quarterly - Monthly and semi-weekly deposit schedules apply to larger withholders - Annual reconciliation (CT-W3) is due by January 31 with W-2s

Unemployment Insurance (UI) is administered by the Connecticut Department of Labor (DOL): - Quarterly wage reports (UC-5A / UC-5B) must be filed with the CT DOL - Nonprofit employers may elect reimbursable employer status — confirm with CT DOL

Connecticut Paid Leave (CT PFL): Connecticut operates a paid family and medical leave program. Employers are required to withhold CT PFL contributions from employee wages and remit them to the Connecticut Paid Leave Authority quarterly. This is a payroll deduction from employees (employers do not contribute separately in most cases), but employers are responsible for withholding correctly and remitting on the proper schedule.

Operational takeaway: Connecticut's payroll compliance layer includes DRS, CT DOL, and CT Paid Leave Authority — three different agencies. Confirm you have accounts in all applicable systems and know each quarterly deadline.

3) Federal church filing requirement nuance

IRS guidance states that tax-exempt organizations generally file annual returns, but churches and some church-affiliated organizations may be excepted.

Operational takeaway: Don't assume "all churches file 990" or "no churches file anything." Validate your exact entity/facts with your tax advisor.

Monthly church compliance checklist (practical)

  • Review upcoming 90-day deadlines (state + payroll + governance)
  • Confirm owner and status for each open item
  • Verify any org info changes that require updated filings with SOTS
  • Confirm payroll reporting/deposit workflow is on track (DRS, DOL, CT PFL)
  • Capture board decisions/minutes and any policy updates
  • Verify volunteer screening/training due dates

Common failure patterns we keep seeing

  1. Compliance is tracked in scattered spreadsheets with no owner accountability
  2. Charitable registration renewal missed because it's on a different deadline than the SOTS annual report
  3. CT Paid Leave withholding not set up correctly when hiring new employees
  4. No monthly review ritual, so deadlines are discovered late
